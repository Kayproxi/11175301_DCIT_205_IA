The Computer Science Department of the University of Ghana is proud to announce the following upcoming events for the academic year 2023/2024. These events are open to all students, faculty, staff, and alumni of the department, as well as the general public. Please mark your calendars and join us for these exciting opportunities to learn, network, and have fun.

Computer Science Week : This is an annual celebration of computer science and its impact on society. It will take place from Monday, October 2, 2023 to Friday, October 6, 2023. The week will feature various activities, such as guest lectures, panel discussions, workshops, exhibitions, and competitions. The theme for this year is “Computing for Good”, and the keynote speaker will be Dr. Regina Honu, the founder and CEO of Soronko Solutions, a social enterprise that empowers young women and girls through technology.

Career Fair: This is a one-day event where students can meet and interact with potential employers and recruiters from various sectors and industries that require computer science skills. It will take place on Wednesday, January 24, 2024 at the Great Hall. The event will include a job fair, a resume clinic, a mock interview session, and a networking lunch. Some of the participating organizations include Microsoft, Google, IBM, MTN, Ecobank, and Ghana Health Service.

Research Symposium: This is a two-day event where students and faculty can showcase their research projects and publications in computer science. It will take place from Thursday, March 15, 2024 to Friday, March 16, 2024 at the Conference Center. The event will include oral and poster presentations, a keynote speech, and an award ceremony. The keynote speaker will be Prof. Kwabena Boahen, a renowned computer scientist and neuroscientist from Stanford University.

We hope you will join us for these events and support our department’s vision of advancing computer science education and research in Ghana and beyond. For more information and registration, please visit our website or contact us via email or phone. Thank you for your interest in our department.
